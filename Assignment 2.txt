Features of the Hans Rosling Visualisation:

Task1:

1. A bubble plot visualization which represents the countries of the world
2. Countries of the world described by GDP and Global Competitive Index mapped to x and y-axis position.
3. The population of each country mapped to the bubble area. As the years slide through, the size of the bubble changes as per the country population figure.
4. To start the visualization, press on the start button to animate the changes in country statistics throughout the years. To pause, press on the pause button.
5. To view data for a particular country and a particular year, hover over the bubble representing the countries. It displays the name of the country, The GDP data, Global Competitiveness Index data,   Region and Population.
6. Each Region is assigned to a particular color used to differentiate between the regions and the legend for the same is represented on the righthand side of the chart.
7. Slider feature to change the values through years 2007-2017. Click and drag the slider to change years manually.


Task2:

1. To view the trace of a country over the years in static view, click on the pause button and then click on the particular country. To continue the animation, press on the start button.
2. The second visualization shows a bar chart representation used to compare the 12 pillars of the Global Competitiveness Index.
3. The values for the pillars are shown as a cumulative average throughout the years(2007-2017).
4. Click on the drop-down and select the country for which you wish to view the data.
